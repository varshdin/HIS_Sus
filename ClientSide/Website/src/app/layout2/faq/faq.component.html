ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) aspects have become increasingly important for consumers and investors. Firms need to think not only of stakeholders who value their financial performance but also of those who want to understand their positive and negative contributions to sustainability. Policymakers worldwide are also reacting and requiring firms to disclose information about their ESG performance. As a result, understanding and improving their ESG footprint became a priority for many firms. Building a sustainable business helps firms to create long-term value for their stakeholders.

A sound risk management plan is structured and must cover relevant processes and risk sources that are both internal and external to the organization. Many economic, environmental, geopolitical, technological, and social forces create risks that can affect firm's performance. Proactively identifying and managing these risks is critical for a firm's long-term success.

The United Nations Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI) is an international network of financial institutions that promotes the integration of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) aspects into investment decisions. The PRI reporting is the largest global project on responsible investment. Signatories must report annually on their responsible investment activities and follow six principles to promote a more sustainable global financial system.

There are several benefits associated with measuring and reporting emissions at different levels. Firms can use this information to communicate with stakeholders and identify opportunities to increase resource efficiency in their processes and supply chain. By analyzing their emissions footprint and understanding their main drivers, firms can also foster innovation, such as the development of new products, services, or business models. For many firms, Scope 3 emissions represent the most significant impact. They are, however, much more challenging to measure, as they require information about emissions along the entire value chain.
